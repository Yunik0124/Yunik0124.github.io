RIZAL’S FIRST TRAVEL ABROAD

His first trip abroad and his secret mission He must get the best education he could find and answer his life question:
What could he do to help his oppressed Motherland? He has to study in Madrid but this plan was kept secret.

His first trip abroad and his secret mission to observe keenly the life and culture, languages and customs, industries
and government and laws of European nations in order to prepare himself of the mighty task of liberating his
oppressed people from Spanish tyranny.

MAY 1888 TO MARCH 1889

RIzal lived on London to improved his knowledge on English, to study
Dr. Antonio de Morga’s book SUCCESOS DE LAS ISLAS FILIPINAS, which
is found in the British Museum.

MARCH 1889

Too many tourists in the city, so he stayed with Valentin Ventura. He
transferred from different hotels and boarding houses. He visited the
home of Pardo de Tavera. Composed the story of THE MONKEY AND
THE TORTOISE.

MAY 6 1889

He was fascinated by the Universal Exposition, especially the Eiffel
Tower. He organized the Kidlat Club, with Filipinos who wanted to join
the expo. He finalized his copy of Morga’s Book which showed
evidence that the Filipinos were already civilized before the
Spaniards came.

JANUARY 28 1890

He left for Brussels, and arrived in Belgium. Jose Albert accompanied
him to Brussels. He also lived with Jose Alejandro. He Paris because
of expensive cost of living.

AUGUST 1890

Rizal arrived in Madrid. But bad news pilled as he arrived in the
country. Failure to get justice for the family. The death of Jose Maria
Panganiban, his propaganda comrade, in Barcelona due to illness
on August 19 1890.

JANUARY 1 1891

The Filipinos moved to recognized the Association Hispano Filipina.
Rizal scouted around to look for a suitable printing shop, he finally
found one in Ghent, the Meyer Van Loo Press which agreed ti print the
book while being in pad in installment.

OCTOBER 18 1891

Rizal boarded the steamer Melbourne in Marseilles and arrived in
Hong Kong on November 20 1891. While in Hong Kong he wrote a letter
to Queen Regent Maria Cristina. Explaining to her situation in
Calamba.
