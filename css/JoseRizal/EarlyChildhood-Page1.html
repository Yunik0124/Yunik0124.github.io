Rizal's Early Childhood Life

He was the seventh child of Fancisco Rizal Mercado and Teodora Alonzo y Quintos. Jose grew up in a wealthy
family and was a child prodigy. He learned the alphabet by the age of 3. He could both read and write by the age
of 5.

JUNE 22 1861

The Church baptistery, where Rizal baptized on June 22, 1861 by the
then parish priest of Calamba Rufino Collantes and his grandfather
Pedro Casanas, is a recognized National Historical Landmark.

1864

He learned the alphabet from his Mother at 3, and could read and
write at age 5.

1865-1867

During this time his mother taught him how to read and write. His
father hired a classmate by the name of Leon Monroy for five
months until Leon Monroy death, taught Rizal the rudiments of
Latin.
About this time his mother’s cousin frequented Calamba. Uncle Manuel
Alberto, seeing Rizal frail in body, concerned himself with the
physical development of his young nephew and taught the latter
love for the open ari and developed in him a great admiration
for the beauty of nature, while uncle Gregorio, a scholar,
instilled into the mind of the boy love for education.
