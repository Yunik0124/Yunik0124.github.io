Rizal's Educational Background

Rizal first studied under Justiniano Aquino Cruz in Binan, Laguna, before he was sent to Manila.
He took the entrance examination to Colegio de San Juan de Letran, as his father requested, but he
he enrolled at the Ateneo Municipal de Manila. He graduated as one of the nine students in his
class declared sobresaliente or outstanding.

He continued his education at the Ateneo Municipal de Manila to obtain a land surveyor and assessor's
degreeand simultaneously at the University of Santo Tomas, where he studied a preparatory course in law
and finished with a mark of excelente, or excellent. He finished the course of Philosophy as a pre-law.

1861-1870

Jose begins school under the instruction of Justiniano Aquin Cruz at
age of 19. 1871 Jose continues his education under the instruction
of Lucas Padua.

1872-1877

At the age of eleven, Rizal started attending the Ateneo Municipal
de Manila for his secondary education. He excelled in classes
including philosophy, physics, chemistry, and natural history.
During his time at this institution, he wrote poetry that was
recognised with awards, read books, and even wrote a play titled
“Junto al Pasig”. He also pursued his lifelong interests in
painting, clay modelling, and drawing.

1877-1882

Rizal eventually earned a degree in land surveying and assessment
from the Ateneo Municipal while attending the University of Santo
Tomas to study philosophy and literature. In 1878, he also
enrolled in medical school. He also travelled to Europe to study
at Universidad Central de Madrid for his Licentiate in Philosophy
and Letters and Opthalmology.

1883-1884

Following the completion of his medical studies, he began visiting
nations in Europe and Asia. Spain was the first nation he travelled
to at the time, then Singapore, Ceylon (now known as Sri Lanka),
Italy, France, Barcelona, Paris, Madrid, and so on.

1885-1887

Rizal earned a Licentiate in Medicine from the Universidad Central
de Madrid, where he also studied literature and philosophy. While
he was in Madrid, he had the inspiration for Noli Me Tangere. In
1887, he completed his eye-specific training at the University
of Heidelberg and continued his studies at the University of
Paris. It was also the year that Rizal’s first book was
published in Berlin.

1887-1888

Rizal went back to the Philippines in order to continue his career.
In addition to treating young Filipinos and imparting European
culture, he was excited to treat his mother’s eyes. He then
travelled to London on a further European tour to carry out
historical studies on the Philippines.
