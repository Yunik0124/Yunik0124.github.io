RIZAL’S FIRST TRAVEL ABROAD

His first trip abroad and his secret mission He must get the best education he could find and answer his life question:
What could he do to help his oppressed Motherland? He has to study in Madrid but this plan was kept secret.

His first trip abroad and his secret mission to observe keenly the life and culture, languages and customs, industries
and government and laws of European nations in order to prepare himself of the mighty task of liberating his
oppressed people from Spanish tyranny.

JUNE 15 1882

He left Marseilles for Barcelona in an express train. Rizal crossed the
Pyreness and stopped for a day at the frontier town of Port Bou.

JUNE 16 1882

At 12:00 noon, Rizal arrived at Barcelona and boarded in the Fonda
De Espana. Rizal first impression of Barcelona, the greatest city of
Cataluna and Spain’s second largest city was unfavorable.

LIFE IN BARCELONA

He wrote an essay entitled “AMOR PATRIO” or Love Country. His next
article was entitled “LOS VIAJES” or Travels and followed by “REVISTA
DE MADRID” or Review of Madrid but the latter was returned because
the publication was ceased because of lack of funds.

AUGUST 20 1882

His article “AMOR PATRIO” was published in the Diarong Tagalog,
a Manila newspaper edited by Basilio Teodoro. This was the First
article he wrote abroad.

SEPTEMBER 2 1882

Rizal moved into abroad in order to continue his medical studies.
Rizal enrolled at the Unibersidad Central de Madrid. In two courses.
Medicine and Philosophy and Letters, November 3 1882.

LIFE IN MADRID

Academy of Fine Arts of San Fernando Rizal studied painting and
sculpture. Took lesson in French, German and English under private
instructors. Practiced fencing and shooting at the Hall of Arms in
Sanz y Carbonell.

CIRCULO-HOSPANO FILIPINO

It is an association formed by Filipino students in Spain composed
both Filipinos and Spaniards. Me Piden Versos or They Ask Me For
Verses upon the request of the members of this society, Rizal wrote
this poem which his personally declaimed during New Year's Eve
reception

MARCH 1883

He joined the Masonic Lodge called ACACIA. His reason for was to
secure Freemasonry’s aid in his fight in the Philippines. November
1883, He transferred to LODGE SOLIDARIDAD, where he become a
Master Mason
