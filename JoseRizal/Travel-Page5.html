RIZAL'S SECOND TRAVEL ABROAD

Hounded by enemies by enemies and threatened by friars, Rizal was forced to leave in Philippines
for the second time. Rizal at 27 was an embittered victim of human iniquities, dilusioned dreamer,
and a frustrated reformer.

FEBRUARY 3 1888

Rizal left the Philippines half-sick and disillusion. Zafiro the vessel
he rides on. The vessel arrived in Amoy or Xiamen, China but Rizal
did not land because of his frail health, the city’s rainy weather and
the report that the city was dirty.

FEBRUARY 22 1888

Rizal left Hongkong on board the OCEANIC. His next desitination
was Japan.

FEBRUARY 28 1888

He arrived at Yokohama and he stayed at the Grand Hotel. The
next day he went to Tokyo and stayed there from March 2-7. Later
he moved over to the Spanish legation on invitation since staying
with the Spaniards would save him money and believed that he had
nothing to hide from them.

APRIL 13 1888

Rizal left Yokohama on board the streamer BELGIC bound for USA.

APRIL 18 1888

Rizal boarded the English vessel, Belgic

APRIL 28 1888

Rizal reached SAN FRANCISCO. Dubbed America as “A motherland
for the poor who wish to work”. He described America as a great
country, but it has many defects too. They do not have real civil
liberty.

MAY 13 1888

Rizal was arrived in New York and he called New York the Big Town.
Rizal impression about America is Positive, America is land of great
opportunity especially for immigrants. It has a huge farms and
factories.

MAY 16 1888

Rizal left New York and arrived in Liverpool on May 24 1888 and
stayed there for a day before moving to London where he stayed
as a guest in a house of Antonio Ma. Regidor.
